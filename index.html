Dylan Hutcheson

About

Dylan Hutcheson is a violist and conductor from the Dallas/Fort Worth area of Texas. He earned his Bachelor of Music Performance in 2026 from the Frost School of Music at the University of Miami. There, he studied viola with Jodi Levitz and conducting with Gerard Schwarz. Dedicated to the art of classical music, he brings a diverse and versatile artistry to the stage.

Viola

Dylan is an accomplished violist. He has experience performing in solo, chamber, and orchestral settings. From the baroque beauty of Bach, to the modern edges of Shostakovich and Elliot Carter, he has put his stamp on the viola's exquisite repertoire. Awarded the position in the Stamps String Quartet, he has also collaborated in other chamber ensemlbes such Oboe Quartet and trio, String Sextets and Octets, and even String and Wind Octet. Dylan's orchestral experience is just as impressive, being given the opportunity to work with some of the finest conductors in the world such as: Gerard Schwarz, Carl St. Claire, Robert Spano, and many others.

Conductor

Dylan is an aspiring conductor. Studying with Gerard Schwarz, he's been given a great mentor to learn from. He's conducted several different ensemlbes, from the Serenades of Dvorak, to the symphonies of Beethoven, Mozart, and Haydn.
